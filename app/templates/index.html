The Problem

Why water quality index
is important?

Health and Human Survival

Clean and safe water is essential for sustaining human life and preventing the spread of diseases such as diarrhea, cholera, typhoid, and hepatitis. Proper sanitation safeguards communities from health risks caused by contaminated water.

Prevention of Global Environmental

According to WHO and UNICEF reports, poor water quality and inadequate sanitation result in millions of deaths annually worldwide, including thousands in Indonesia. Monitoring water quality is a crucial step in reducing these adverse effects.

Resilience to Climate Change

Water acts as a critical link between the environment, ecosystems, climate, and society. Monitoring water quality supports efforts to mitigate and adapt to the impacts of climate change.

Sustainability of Water Resources

Routine monitoring of WQI helps ensure the sustainability of clean water availability, both in quantity and quality, which is a fundamental need for environmental and human life.

Support for Water Purification Technologies

Detecting contamination through WQI monitoring enables the development of water purification technologies, such as distillation and deionization, ensuring water is reused safely and efficiently.

Basis for Decision-Making in Water Management

WQI provides actionable information for identifying water quality issues quickly, aiding decision-making in water resource management and public policy planning.
